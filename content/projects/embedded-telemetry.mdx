## Problem

Agricultural test site needed soil and atmospheric data from 6 locations spread over 2 km. No cell coverage on site. Nodes had to run on battery for an entire growing season.

## Approach

Each node is an ESP32-WROOM-32E + HopeRF RFM95W (SX1276, 915 MHz LoRa). Sensors: SHT31 for temperature/humidity, capacitive soil moisture probe (generic resistive-to-voltage type, calibrated per-unit). Packets are 24 bytes, transmitted at SF7/125 kHz BW on a configurable interval (default 5 min). Between transmissions the ESP32 enters deep sleep (~10 uA).

Gateway runs on a Raspberry Pi 3B+ with an RFM95W hat. Receives packets, timestamps them, and POSTs JSON to an InfluxDB instance over Wi-Fi.

Power source is a single Samsung INR18650-35E (3500 mAh) with a small solar trickle charger. Firmware handles brownout detection and will increase sleep interval if cell voltage drops below 3.3 V.

## Result

6 nodes deployed for 3 months. Packet delivery rate: 98.5%. Average current draw per node: 12 mA including TX bursts. Without solar assist, calculated battery life is ~4 months; with the trickle charger, nodes ran indefinitely through the trial period.
