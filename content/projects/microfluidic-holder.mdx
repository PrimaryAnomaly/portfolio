## Problem

Microfluidic chips in the bioregenerative life support loop needed a repeatable, leak-free mounting interface. The lab was using hand-clamped acrylic fixtures that leaked above ~10 PSI and took 15 minutes per chip swap.

## Approach

Holder and manifold assembly modeled in SolidWorks. Dowel-pin alignment (two-pin datum scheme) for chip registration. Fluid ports use Parker 2-012 O-ring face seals with captive screw compression. Quick-disconnect fittings (Colder PMC series) on all fluid lines. Full GD&T on the drawing package -- position tolerance of 0.05 mm on the port pattern.

Prototyped on a Formlabs Form 3 in Durable resin for fit checks. Final parts CNC-machined from PEEK for chemical and thermal compatibility.

## Result

Held 30 PSI with zero leakage over 72-hour pressure-hold tests. Chip swap time dropped to under 2 minutes. Three units built; one integrated into the CubeSat payload thermal management loop, two in use on the bench.
