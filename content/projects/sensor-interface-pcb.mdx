## Problem

Space-bound biological experiments require simultaneous measurement of pH, dissolved oxygen, lactate, and optical density. Commercial sensor modules are too large, too power-hungry, and not rated for the thermal and vibration environment of a CubeSat payload.

## Approach

Designed a 4-layer PCB in KiCad integrating a multi-channel analog front end with an STM32 microcontroller. The board includes transimpedance amplifiers for electrochemical sensors, LED driver circuits for optical measurements, and a 16-bit ADC for data acquisition. Firmware written in C handles real-time sampling at 1 kHz with DMA transfers.

Fabricated through JLCPCB. Assembled in-house with SMD reflow. Validated against laboratory-grade reference instruments.

## Result

Achieved sub-millivolt noise floor on electrochemical channels. Optical density measurements correlated within 2% of commercial spectrophotometer readings. Board passed thermal cycling (-20C to +60C) and random vibration testing per NASA GEVS. Published in MDPI Sensors (2024).
