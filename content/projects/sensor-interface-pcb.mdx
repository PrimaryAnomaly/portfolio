## Problem

A CubeSat biological payload needed concurrent pH, dissolved oxygen, lactate, and optical density measurements. Nothing off-the-shelf fit the volume, power, or environmental requirements.

## Approach

4-layer PCB designed in KiCad. STM32F4 MCU, four transimpedance amplifier channels (LMP7721-based) for the electrochemical sensors, constant-current LED drivers for OD measurement, and an ADS1115 16-bit ADC. Firmware in C; sampling at 1 kHz using DMA to avoid CPU-bound bottlenecks.

Boards fabricated at JLCPCB, reflowed in-house with solder paste stencil and a modified toaster oven. Each board bench-tested against a Gamry potentiostat and an Agilent 8453 spectrophotometer before integration.

## Result

Electrochemical channels measured under 0.5 mV RMS noise floor. OD readings within 2% of the Agilent reference across 0.1--1.5 AU. Board survived thermal cycling (-20 C to +60 C, 8 cycles) and random vibration (14.1 gRMS) per NASA GEVS. Work published in MDPI Sensors, 2024.
